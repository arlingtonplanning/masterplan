Since the year 2000 the town has slowly gained 450 new residents, and projections show this trend will continue until 2035, when Arlington will have nearly 50,000 residents.1 This population growth has been accompanied by age shifts, with increases in school-aged children and older adults, but losses in the 20-34 age group. As more young families have moved into town and non- family households have moved out, household size has grown slightly after decades of decline. Incomes of town residents have also increased in recent decades. These demographic changes have important fiscal implications, since school-aged children and senior citizens with fixed incomes generally present greater costs to Arlington than working-aged adults, even though income growth may provide more resources to pay property taxes that fund town operations.
